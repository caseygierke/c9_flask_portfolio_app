EDUCATION

 

MS Hydrology 2012

New Mexico Tech, Socorro, NM GPA: 3.76

Thesis title: Sourcing Tree Water in the Sacramento Mountains of New Mexico: A Stable Isotope Study.

This study was part of the Sacramento Mountain Watershed Study, a project aimed to assess the effects of tree thinning on aquifer recharge. My role was to analyze the stable isotopes of tree water samples and match results to samples from possible sources. This information was used to determine if trees use water stored in soils or aquifers in order to understand how they interact with the hydrologic system and if their removal could result in increased groundwater recharge.
